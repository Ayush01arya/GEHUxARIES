Awards

Young Scientist Award (YSA)
The YSA awardees will receive:
• Free registration
• Reimbursement of local accommodation with a ceiling of INR 5000


To qualify applications for the Young Scientist Award (YSA), the applicant must be:
* Less than 35 years of age as on 1 October, 2024
* The principal author of a paper submitted and accepted for oral or poster presentation at a regular session of URSI-RCRS 2024

Applicants from all over the world, including regions that do not (yet) belong to URSI, are welcome. Applicants interested in promoting contacts between developed and developing countries are highly encouraged. Applications should be completed electronically on the URSI-RCRS 2024 conference website by the last date (link). Every YSA applicant must submit the following documents in PDF format, through the Paper submission webpage:

• Full-length paper (4 pages) in IEEE Format
• Full CV
• Complete List of Publications separately mentioning Journals & Conference Proceedings

The URSI-RCRS 2024 Young Scientist Program (YSP) Committee will peer-review the applications, and select the awardees at the end. The YSP Committee will notify all the applicants of the review results by email on 01 ………..2024. Within three days of notification, every YSA awardees must confirm that he/she will attend URSI-RCRS 2024. By 31 ……… 2024, the list of the YSA awardees will be announced on the URSI-RCRS 2024 website.

The YSA awardees are required to attend the official Conference Banquet, where the awardees will be announced. All the YSA awardees are also required to attend the URSI-RCRS 2020 Student Paper Competition (SPC) Special Session, where they will be asked to contribute to evaluations of the presentations by the five SPC finalists.
The list of the YSA candidates is available here.

Student Paper Competition (SPC)

Full-time university/institute students from any country, who is the principal (first-named) author and the presenter of a paper (oral or poster) at a regular session of URSI-RCRS 2024, are eligible for application in the SPC.


Applications should be completed electronically on the URSI-RCRS 2024 conference website by last date (link). For SPC submission the following documents (in PDF format) must be submitted simultaneously through the paper submission portal:
• Full-length paper (4 pages) IEEE paper style: The instructions given on the conference website must be followed for preparing this.
• Electronically scanned Letter of Certification from his/her advisor(s):
This should be printed on the university/institute letterhead and signed by the applicant’s advisor, clearly stating that the applicant is a full-time student for a degree at the time of application. For a paper with multiple authors, the letter should emphasize that the role of the authors other than the applicant was primarily advisory in nature.

The URSI-RCRS 2024 Young Scientist and Student Program (YSSP) Committee will peer-review the SPC applications. Five finalists will be selected at the end of the review process. The YSSP Committee will email all the SPC applicants on 7 ……..2024, notifying them about the review results. On 31 ……..2024, the list of the SPC finalists will be announced on the URSI-RCRS 2024 website. Within two weeks of the notification, every finalist must confirm that he/she will attend URSI-RCRS 2024.

The SPC Special Session, open to all URSI-RCRS 2024 participants, will be scheduled during the conference duration. All the five finalists should –
(i) Present their papers orally in the SPC Special Session
(ii) Present their papers in regular sessions (oral or poster)
The finalists also have to attend the official Conference Banquet, where the winners will be announced. At the SPC Special Session, members of the URSI-RCRS 2024 YSSP Committee will judge presentations by the five finalists and select the three winners (1st, 2nd, and 3rd prizes), who will receive: 

                    * 1st prize: Certificate and INR 5000
                    * 2nd prize: Certificate and INR 3000
                    * 3rd prize: Certificate and INR 2000

The two non-winning finalists will each receive “Honorable Mention” certificate identifying him/her as a finalist at the Conference Banquet.
